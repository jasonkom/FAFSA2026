FAFSA 2026–27: Every Major Change Explained (November 2025 Update)

The 2026–27 Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) launched on September 24, 2025 — a full week ahead of the traditional October 1 date. This is the first full year of the Student Aid Index (SAI) replacing the Expected Family Contribution (EFC), plus brand-new asset exemptions from the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (Pub. L. 119-21, signed July 4, 2025).

The 7 Biggest Changes for 2026–27

  1. SAI fully replaces EFC – can now be negative down to –1,500 (max Pell $7,395)
  2. No more sibling discount – multi-child families may see less aid per student
  3. Family farms & small businesses (≤100 employees) completely exempt – huge win for rural & entrepreneurial families
  4. Foreign earned income now counted for Pell eligibility
  5. Only delineated 401(k)/IRA contributions reported as untaxed income
  6. Form reduced to under 50 questions for most families
  7. Immediate SSN verification & email contributor invites
